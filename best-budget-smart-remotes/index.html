Quick answer

The BroadLink RM4 Pro is our editor’s pick for the best budget smart remote because it combines voice control, IR blasting, and smart-home hub features at a very accessible price.

The Amazon Fire TV Voice Remote Pro is the most popular choice for cord-cutters who live in the Fire TV ecosystem, while the One For All URC7980 is a strong runner-up for anyone who just wants a straightforward universal voice remote without extra smart-home bells.

A universal smart TV remote can replace the clutter of three or four clickers and add voice search, which makes finding shows or adjusting volume feel almost magical. On a budget, the trick is to find a remote that covers the devices you actually own (TV, soundbar, streaming stick) without piling on features you will never use. When shopping, focus on compatibility: IR remotes work with most TVs and audio gear, while Bluetooth or Wi‑Fi models also control streaming boxes and smart‑home devices. Voice assistants matter too, some use Alexa, others Google Assistant or their own proprietary system. The three remotes here each balance cost and capability differently, so the right pick depends on whether you are all‑in on one ecosystem or need to wrangle a mix of old and new gear.
