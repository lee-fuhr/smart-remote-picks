Best for specific needs

Best for whole-home theater control

If you’re juggling a TV, A/V receiver, soundbar, game console, and streaming box, the Sofabaton X1 is the sensible choice. Its combination of IR, Bluetooth, and Wi‑Fi means you can hide everything in a cabinet. Best for voice-first streaming

When your priority is barking commands at Alexa to launch Netflix, pause playback, or search for shows, the Amazon Fire TV Voice Remote Pro is tailor-made. It’s designed exclusively for Fire TV, so the voice integration is seamless. The remote finder feature is a lifesaver if you tend to lose the remote between cushions. Just understand that this is not a universal remote, it’s a streaming companion, and that’s fine if that’s what you need. Our pick: Amazon Fire TV Voice Remote Pro.

Best for a budget-friendly living room

For a simple setup with a TV, soundbar, and maybe one streaming device, the GE Universal Voice Remote delivers surprising value. It gives you voice control for a fraction of the cost of flagship remotes. The IR-only limitation is real, you’ll need to point it at devices, but if your gear sits on a shelf in plain sight, that’s not a problem. It’s the right pick for small spaces or second rooms where you don’t want to invest heavily. Our pick: GE Universal Remote Control with Voice Control for up to 4 Devices.

Our verdict

No single remote works for every setup. If you want one remote to truly replace all others and you value future-proofing, the Sofabaton X1 is our top recommendation. For hardcore home-theater enthusiasts who don’t mind a discontinued product, the Logitech Harmony Elite still delivers unmatched flexibility. And if you’re on a budget or just need a better streaming clicker, the GE Voice Remote and Amazon Fire TV Remote Pro are excellent picks for those specific niches. Focus on the number of devices you control, whether you need a hub for hidden gear, and your tolerance for setup quirks. With that in mind, you’ll find the right match.
